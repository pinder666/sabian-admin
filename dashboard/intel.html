SPEED + NO DEFAULT

MALAYSIA 1998
CAPITAL CONTROLS
Ringgit under attack, capital flight, stock market −75%. Rejected IMF. Pegged currency Sep 1, 1998. Blocked offshore ringgit trading — eliminated currency attack mechanism entirely.
Same Sabian signals as Thailand/Indonesia. Single-day policy decision broke the pattern.
URUGUAY 2003
CACs + VOLUNTARY
Argentina contagion, GDP −11%, bank run. 93% participation in 3-month voluntary exchange. CACs bound the 7% holdouts — no pari passu veto, no Elliott play possible.
Uruguay had identical signals to Argentina. Escape mechanism: contract law, not economics.
POLAND 1994
CREDIBILITY ANCHOR
$48.5B communist debt, 640% hyperinflation, GDP −9%. Brady Plan covered all commercial debt at once — no loose claims. EU accession path kept bonds above distressed pricing.
Same signals as Russia 1998 (which produced massive extraction). EU anchor was the difference.
JAMAICA 2010 / 2013
JURISDICTIONAL WALL
140% debt-to-GDP — worse than Greece when Greece was extracted. Two restructurings, zero vulture litigation. Debt was Jamaican law, held by Jamaican banks and pension funds. Elliott has no claim in Kingston.
JDX 99% participation (2010). NDX2 99.5% participation (2013). Zero external default.
Six Escape Patterns Structural factors that break the extraction mechanism
A — NO DEFAULT
Voluntary exchange while bonds still above 50¢. No default = no distressed entry price = no vulture play.
Korea · Uruguay · Jamaica
B — SPEED
Restructuring with hard deadline + pre-committed majority completes before accumulation is possible.

C — COLLECTIVE ACTION CLAUSES
CACs bind holdouts to majority votes. Eliminates the individual veto that makes the Elliott play work.
Uruguay · Now in most new sovereign bonds post-2003
D — JURISDICTIONAL WALL
Domestic debt under domestic law, held domestically. No SDNY filing. No UK court. No international leverage.
Jamaica — 140% debt-to-GDP, zero litigation
E — CAPITAL CONTROLS
Blocking capital movement eliminates the currency attack mechanism. Same event = crisis signal AND potential escape.
Malaysia — Sep 1, 1998 peg announcement
F — CREDIBILITY ANCHOR
External institutional anchor (EU accession, regional integration) maintains bond prices above distressed levels and creates political durability for reform.
Poland — EU accession path vs Russia (no anchor)
CURRENT DATA GAP — STRUCTURAL OVERLAYS
Sabian reads vulnerability. It does not determine outcome. Two countries with identical signal profiles can produce opposite outcomes based on debt composition (domestic vs. external ratio, governing law), CAC presence in bond documentation, and institutional anchor strength. Adding these structural overlays — from IMF Article IV reports and World Bank International Debt Statistics — upgrades the product from diagnosis to diagnosis + prescription.
