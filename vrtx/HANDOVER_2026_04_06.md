## 2. Line 8 → 9 → 10 Cascade Rule

### What Line 8 establishes

Line 8 (Sabian, index 7) delivers the adenosine cascade when the governing mechanism is incomplete adenosine clearance:

> Caffeine blocks the adenosine signal but doesn't clear it. When it wears off, the remaining adenosine becomes noticeable again, and the common follow-on is a reach for quick energy — sugar or simple carbohydrates. That cycle makes alertness harder to maintain across the day.

**Caffeine in Line 8 is an EXAMPLE of stimulation-seeking — not the governing mechanism.**

The cascade is:
```
early stimulation
→ adenosine signal masked
→ accumulation continues underneath
→ wears off
→ rebound pressure surfaces
→ reach for quick energy (sugar / carbs)
→ alertness degrades across the day
```

### What Line 9 establishes (Host A, index 8)

Line 9 names the biological consequence of the cascade and demands one move. It does NOT name the intervention. It does NOT reference caffeine.

Compliant form:
> "So the low resting heart rate buys Jason a steady platform, but the sleep gap sets up a stimulation trap later. What's the move?"

### What Line 10 must do (Sabian, index 9)

Line 10 **breaks the cascade**. It does not complete the caffeine narrative from Lines 6–8.

**The prescription is independent of substance.** Whether the user drinks caffeine, tea, pre-workout, or nothing — the cascade is the same. The action is the same.

**Compliant forms:**
- `"Don't start the cascade — let the pressure clear on its own before reaching for anything to wake you up."`
- `"Wait until midday before using anything to unnaturally wake yourself up — letting adenosine clear first prevents the crash later."`

**Non-compliant forms:**
- Any sentence naming caffeine, coffee, tea, or any specific substance
- Any sentence with a numeric timing claim not grounded in the retrieved corpus (e.g., "90 minutes", "ninety minutes")
- Any sentence with two actions joined by "and"
- Any sentence with a framing phrase before the action verb ("The move is to...")

### Why the model keeps drifting

Root cause: 9 lines of in-context caffeine discussion create a strong in-context prior. The model's generation of Line 10 is conditioned on that full conversational context — it completes the caffeine narrative by momentum, not by rule. Correction messages in the retry loop cannot reliably override a 9-line prior.

The fix attempted at session end: restructured the Line 10 block in `buildUserPrompt` to place the CASCADE ANCHOR first, remove all caffeine-referencing compliant/non-compliant examples, and reduce total instruction volume. **This restructure is in the code but has not yet produced a clean run.**

Both violations fired on both attempts. Engine proceeded with the failing Line 10.

### Grounding gate outcome

After the retry loop, `groundLine10Claims` detected "90 minutes" but found no corpus boundary (`before noon`, `before midday`, `mid-morning`) in the retrieved knowledge. Gate logged:

> `[VRTX] Grounding gate: timing claim "90 minutes" survived — no corpus boundary to replace with. Passing through.`

Result: failing Line 10 passed through to the script output unchanged.

### Why the grounding gate cannot fix this

The gate is replace-only. It substitutes a timing claim with a grounded corpus boundary. If the corpus has no boundary to substitute, the gate passes through. It does NOT strip or rewrite.

The substance problem (`caffeine`) is not handled by the gate at all — gate only handles timing.

## 4. Grounding Gate Status

**File:** `vrtx/engine/vrtx_engine.cjs`, function `groundLine10Claims` (~line 1274)

**Current behavior:**
- Runs after the validator retry loop on the final contracted lines
- Detects timing claims in Line 10 (numeric and written-out: `\b(?:\d+|thirty|forty[-\s]?five|sixty|ninety|...)\s*(?:minute|hour)s?\b`)
- Searches the retrieved knowledge corpus for boundary expressions: `before noon`, `until midday`, `by mid-morning`, etc.
- If corpus has 0 boundaries → logs and passes through (no modification)
- If corpus has 1 boundary → replaces timing claim with that boundary
- If corpus has multiple boundaries → replaces with most frequent boundary

**What was removed (strip path):** A prior version attempted to strip the timing claim from the sentence using regex. This was removed because partial regex surgery left dangling prepositions ("delay it by, let..." — "by" orphaned). Strip path is gone. Replace-only remains.

**Current gap:** Retrieved knowledge corpus does not consistently contain `before noon / midday / mid-morning` boundary expressions. When it doesn't, the gate has nothing to substitute and passes the failing claim through. This is the known failure mode.
