# ECUADOR 2008 — EXTRACTION MAP

## CRISIS SUMMARY
President Rafael Correa declared debt "illegitimate" after official audit. Default on $3.2B in bonds. Government bought back 70% of defaulted debt at ~30 cents. China emerged as primary financier post-default, securing long-term oil commitments.

## THE CORREA PLAYBOOK

### 2007: Debt Audit
- Correa commissioned expert audit of Ecuador's debt
- **Finding**: "Most of the country's debt illegitimate because it had been contracted against the interest of the population"

### December 2008: Strategic Default
- Declared default on "illegitimate" debt
- **Rationale**: Prior governments contracted debt corruptly, population shouldn't pay
- **Market reaction**: Bond prices collapsed to ~30 cents

### 2009: Buyback
- Ecuador bought back ~70% of outstanding defaulted bonds
- **Purchase price**: ~30 cents on dollar
- **Savings**: ~70% reduction in debt stock
- **Pattern**: Declare illegitimate → crash prices → buy back cheap

---

## STRATEGIC POSITIONING (Pre-Stress)

### Who Lost
- Original bondholders took 70% haircut if they sold in buyback
- Anyone who held through got nothing until buyback offer

### Who Might Have Positioned
- Distressed debt funds that saw the play coming could have:
  - Shorted pre-default
  - Bought at 20-30 cents, sold into buyback at 30 cents
  - Held out for future litigation

---

## POST-DEFAULT: CHINA ENTERS

### China as Lender of Last Resort
- **Post-2008**: China emerged as Ecuador's primary financier
- **Total Chinese lending**: $11B+ since 2008
- **Sectors**: Infrastructure, oil, mining, energy

### Oil-for-Loan Arrangements
- **PetroChina deal**: Ecuador committed 96,000 barrels/day to Chinese firms
- **Pattern**: Default on Western debt → pivot to Chinese lending → pledge oil as repayment

### The Trap
- Ecuador lost access to Western capital markets post-default
- China stepped in with conditioned lending
- Oil production committed for decades
- **Estimate**: Ecuador "harmed by almost $5 billion" from unfavorable Chinese terms

## UNIQUE FEATURES

### The "Illegitimate Debt" Strategy
- Only case in dataset where government explicitly audited and rejected debt as illegitimate
- Legal theory: Odious debt doctrine — debt contracted by corrupt government against population's interest
- **Result**: Successful default without vulture fund litigation (unlike Argentina)

### China Pivot
- Ecuador's post-default experience shows the pattern:
  1. Default on Western debt
  2. Western markets close
  3. China offers alternative financing
  4. Terms: Oil/resource commitments
  5. Long-term extraction exceeds original debt
