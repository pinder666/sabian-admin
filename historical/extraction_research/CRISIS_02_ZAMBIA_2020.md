# ZAMBIA 2020 — EXTRACTION MAP

## CRISIS SUMMARY
First African sovereign to default during COVID-19 era (November 2020). First major test of G-20 Common Framework for debt restructuring. Tripartite negotiation: Paris Club + Chinese state banks + international bondholders.

## STRATEGIC POSITIONING (Pre-Stress)

### China Development Bank / Exim Bank
- **2010s**: Accumulated infrastructure loans "faster than productive capacity they were meant to unlock"
- Pattern: Loan accumulation during commodity boom, debt trap during bust

### Glencore
- **Pre-2021**: Owned Mopani Copper Mines for over a decade
- **Track record**: Transfer pricing allegations, pollution, mass layoffs
- **2021**: Sold to ZCCM-IH (state-owned) for $1.5B
- **Terms**: Zambia pays Glencore 3% of Mopani's gross revenue 2021-2023
- **Pattern**: Exit distressed asset, maintain revenue extraction via royalty stream

---

## INSTITUTIONAL EXIT

### Pre-Default Positioning
- International bondholders reduced exposure as copper prices stayed "structurally suppressed"
- Capital flight accelerated with COVID-19 fiscal shock

### Restructuring Outcome
- **90%+** of $3B bondholders backed restructuring
- **$840M** in claim reductions
- **$2.5B** cashflow relief across IMF program period

---

## DISTRESSED ENTRY
- G-20 Common Framework process — unprecedented tripartite negotiation
- No major holdout litigation (unlike Argentina) — cooperative restructuring

---

## RESOURCE/ASSET CAPTURE

### Mopani Copper Mine
- **Asset**: One of Africa's largest copper operations
- **Seller**: Glencore + First Quantum Minerals
- **Buyer**: ZCCM-IH (Zambian state)
- **Price**: $1.5B (financed through loan)
- **Revenue share**: 3% of gross revenue to Glencore through 2023
- **Pattern**: Apparent exit, actual continued extraction via revenue royalty

### Copper Borrowing Strategy
- Zambia exploring "copper-for-financing" strategy (copper-linked bonds)
- Pattern: Resource collateralization (same as Chinese oil-for-loans)

## VALUE TRANSFER MAGNITUDE
- **Bondholder haircut**: ~$840M claim reduction
- **Chinese debt**: Restructured under Common Framework
- **Glencore exit value**: $1.5B + ongoing revenue share
- **Root cause**: "Chinese state-linked infrastructure loans accumulated faster than productive capacity"
