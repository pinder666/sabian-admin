## STRATEGIC POSITIONING (Pre-Stress)

### China Development Bank / Exim Bank
- **2010**: Hambantota Port inaugurated — built with China Exim loans
- **2017**: Port commercially nonviable, SLPA unable to service loans
- **2017 (July)**: China Merchants Port Holding acquired 70% stake + 99-year operating lease for $1.1B
- **Pattern**: Infrastructure loan → commercial failure → equity conversion

### Chinese Lending Total
- **2000-2020**: $12B+ in loans for infrastructure projects
- Collateralized by: Hambantota Port, Colombo Port City, highways

## DISTRESSED ENTRY
- Hamilton Reserve Bank (US): Filed lawsuit in US court June 2022, seeking full repayment rather than joining restructuring
- Pattern: Holdout litigation strategy (Elliott playbook)

## RESOURCE/ASSET CAPTURE

### Hambantota Port
- **Asset**: Deep-water port on Indian Ocean shipping lanes (Europe-Africa-Middle East-Asia)
- **Acquirer**: China Merchants Port Holding (Chinese government-owned)
- **Terms**: 70% stake, 99-year lease, $1.1B
- **Timing**: 2017 (pre-full crisis, during debt stress)
- **Strategic value**: Military/commercial dual-use, straddles critical trade routes

---

## VALUE TRANSFER MAGNITUDE
- **Bond haircut**: Restructuring discussions ongoing — estimated 30-50% haircut
- **Port equity transfer**: $1.1B paid for 70% of strategic asset
- **Infrastructure-for-equity**: Pattern of loan → distress → ownership transfer
