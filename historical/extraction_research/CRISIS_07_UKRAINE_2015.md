# UKRAINE 2015 — EXTRACTION MAP

## CRISIS SUMMARY
Debt restructuring during active war with Russia. $15B+ in bonds restructured. Franklin Templeton led bondholder committee holding ~$8.9B (40% of bonds). Final deal: 20% haircut (down from initial 40% demand).

## GEOPOLITICAL CONTEXT

### The War Factor
- February 2014: Russia annexes Crimea
- 2014-2015: War in Donbas
- Ukraine simultaneously fighting and restructuring
- IMF program conditional on debt sustainability

### Russia's $3B Bond
- **December 2013**: Russia loaned $3B to Yanukovych government
- **Terms**: Included clause allowing acceleration if Ukraine's debt exceeded 60% of GDP
- **Status**: Russia refused to participate in restructuring, litigated in UK courts

## RESTRUCTURING DYNAMICS

### Initial Positions
- **Ukraine (Finance Minister Jaresko)**: Demanded 40% haircut
- **Bondholders**: Rejected, sought minimal reduction

### Final Terms (August 2015)
- **Haircut**: 20% (not 40%)
- **Matyours extended**: 4 years
- **GDP-linked warrants**: Upside for bondholders if Ukraine recovers

### IMF Role
- IMF announced it would continue program even without bondholder agreement
- Removed bondholder leverage to hold out

---

## DISTRESSED ENTRY

### Vulture Fund Complications
- **Aurelius Capital + VR Capital**: Sought larger payments than cooperative bondholders
- **Pattern**: Holdout strategy on specific tranches

## VALUE TRANSFER MAGNITUDE
- **Bondholder haircut**: 20% nominal
- **Extended maturities**: 4 years
- **GDP warrants**: Potential upside captured by bondholders if recovery occurs
- **Net transfer**: Ukraine received debt relief; bondholders preserved most principal + recovery upside

## UNIQUE FEATURES

### War During Restructuring
- Only case in dataset where active military conflict coincided with restructuring
- Creditors had limited leverage — Ukraine could credibly threaten "we're fighting a war, sue us later"

### The Russia Bond Anomaly
- $3B Russian loan with sovereign wealth fund status
- Russia refused to participate, litigated in UK
- Geopolitical dimension: creditor is the military aggressor

### GDP Warrants
- Bondholders accepted haircut in exchange for recovery upside
- If Ukraine grows above threshold, bondholders receive additional payments
- Pattern: Risk transfer back to sovereign — heads bondholders win, tails bondholders break even
