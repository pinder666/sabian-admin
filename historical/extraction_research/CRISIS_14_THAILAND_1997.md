# THAILAND 1997 — EXTRACTION MAP

## CRISIS SUMMARY
Origin point of Asian Financial Crisis. Baht collapsed July 2, 1997 after government forced to abandon dollar peg. Hedge funds (Soros et al.) had bet against baht. Triggered regional contagion to Malaysia, Indonesia, South Korea, Philippines. Soros reportedly made $2B profit.

## THE ATTACK

### Hedge Fund Positioning (1996-1997)
- **Soros Fund Management** and similar hedge funds identified:
  - Overvalued baht
  - Unsustainable current account deficit
  - High short-term debt vs. reserves
- **Strategy**: Short the baht, wait for forced devaluation

### The Mechanics
1. Borrow baht
2. Sell baht for dollars
3. Pressure mounts on central bank reserves
4. Central bank defends peg by selling dollars, buying baht
5. Reserves depleted
6. Peg abandoned, baht collapses
7. Repay baht loan with cheaper baht
8. Profit

### July 2, 1997
- Bank of Thailand forced to float the baht
- Immediate 15-20% devaluation
- Eventually fell 50%+ from pre-crisis levels

## UNIQUE FEATURES

### Origin Point
- Thailand was "Patient Zero" for Asian Financial Crisis
- One currency attack triggered regional contagion
- **Lesson**: Currency crises are contagious

### The Soros Model
- Identify fundamentally weak currency
- Build short position with leverage
- Wait for (or pressure) collapse
- Profit on devaluation
- **Refinement of UK 1992 playbook**

### Malaysian Response
- PM Mahathir blamed Soros directly
- Imposed capital controls to prevent speculation
- **Controversial**: Controls worked, but damaged market confidence

### Blame Debate
- **Soros view**: "We didn't cause it, we identified it"
- **Mahathir view**: "Speculators attacked and profited from our misery"
- **Reality**: Vulnerabilities were real; speculators accelerated and profited from collapse
