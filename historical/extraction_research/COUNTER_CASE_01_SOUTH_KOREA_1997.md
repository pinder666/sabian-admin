# COUNTER-CASE 01 — SOUTH KOREA 1997
## "The Country That Broke the Asian Crisis Pattern"

---

## The Setup

South Korea entered the 1997 Asian financial crisis in the same position as every country in the extraction dataset:
- Currency under speculative pressure
- Short-term debt far exceeding reserves
- Banking system exposed to insolvent chaebols
- IMF program required

Every other crisis country in 1997-1998 experienced systematic extraction. South Korea did not.

---

## What Korea Did Differently

### 1. Accepted the IMF — Immediately and Completely
Unlike Indonesia (which resisted and negotiated), Korea signed the IMF agreement in December 1997 and began implementation within weeks. The $58B program ($21B IMF + additional bilateral) was the largest in IMF history at the time.

### 2. Restructured the Chaebols Proactively
Korea did not wait for markets to force asset sales. The government:
- Forced the "Big Deals" — swapping businesses between chaebols to create viable units
- Allowed Daewoo to collapse (1999) rather than prolong zombie financing
- Forced mergers in steel, electronics, auto sectors
- Removed 55 of the 64 largest chaebol affiliates through forced consolidation

### 3. Opened the Capital Account Fully
Rather than imposing capital controls (Malaysia's approach), Korea opened to foreign investment. This allowed foreign buyers to acquire Korean assets — but on terms Korea negotiated, not distressed terms vultures exploit.

### 4. Paid Off the IMF Early — By 2001
Korea repaid the full IMF program 3 years ahead of schedule. This was a deliberate signal to markets that the country had not merely survived but had corrected the structural conditions that caused the crisis.

### 5. Maintained Institutional Continuity
Despite a presidential election in December 1997 (at the peak of the crisis), the incoming Kim Dae-jung government continued and accelerated reforms rather than reversing them. Political continuity of policy prevented the negotiating vacuum that extractors exploit.

---

## Why Extractors Did Not Succeed

### No Default
Korea never defaulted on sovereign bonds. Without a default, there is no distressed debt to buy at 10-30 cents. The vulture play requires a default to enter.

### CACs and Coordinated Restructuring
Where restructuring occurred (banking sector), it was managed domestically with the government controlling the process. Foreign creditors were not given uncoordinated access to individual claims.

### Speed Eliminated the Window
The restructuring was completed faster than extractors could position. By the time Elliott-style entities identified Korea as a target, the recovery was underway. The extraction window — the period of maximum desperation when assets are available at distressed prices — was compressed to months rather than years.

### No Political Vacuum
Argentina's 2001 crisis was extended by political instability (5 presidents in 2 weeks). Korea's institutional continuity meant there was no period where no one was governing. Extractors exploit governing vacuums.

## What Sabian Would Have Shown

Korea in 1996-1997 would have shown ELEVATED economic and capital flow signals — the same signals that preceded Argentina 2001 and Greece 2009. The difference is not detectable in the signals. The signals showed vulnerability. What differed was the response.

**The signal Sabian reads is the window opening. The escape happened because the government closed the window before extractors fully positioned.**
