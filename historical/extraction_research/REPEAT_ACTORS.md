# REPEAT ACTORS — Cross-Crisis Analysis

Across 15 sovereign crises (1994-2022), the same actors appear repeatedly. This document catalogs who appears, how often, and in what role.

## ACTOR TYPOLOGY

### Type A: Vulture Funds (Litigation Strategy)
- **Players**: Elliott, Aurelius, Dart, NML Capital
- **Strategy**: Buy distressed → Refuse restructuring → Litigate → 100 cents
- **Returns**: 300-1,270%
- **Timeline**: 5-15 years patience

### Type B: State Lenders (Resource Extraction)
- **Players**: China CDB/Exim, Rosneft, Gulf SWFs
- **Strategy**: Lend during baseline → Crisis converts debt to equity/resources
- **Returns**: Strategic assets (ports, mines, oil)
- **Timeline**: Decades

### Type C: Institutional Holders (Restructuring Participation)
- **Players**: BlackRock, T. Rowe, PIMCO, Franklin Templeton
- **Strategy**: Hold diversified EM debt → Participate in restructurings → Accept haircut
- **Returns**: Negative on individual crisis, positive on portfolio
- **Timeline**: Continuous exposure

### Type D: Professional Restructurers
- **Players**: Greylock Capital
- **Strategy**: Advise on restructurings, sit on committees
- **Returns**: Fees + positioned investments
- **Timeline**: Per-crisis engagement

### Type E: Currency Speculators
- **Players**: Soros, Tiger, macro hedge funds
- **Strategy**: Short overvalued currencies → Profit on collapse
- **Returns**: Billions on successful attacks
- **Timeline**: Months to years of positioning

## THE EXTRACTION ECOSYSTEM

```
PRE-CRISIS:
  State Lenders (China, Russia) → Infrastructure loans → Collateral secured
  
CRISIS ONSET:
  Currency Speculators → Short positions → Accelerate collapse
  Institutional Holders → Begin exiting → Capital flight
  
CRISIS PEAK:
  Vulture Funds → Buy distressed debt at 10-30 cents
  State Lenders → Convert loans to equity/resources
  
POST-CRISIS:
  Vulture Funds → Litigate for 100 cents (5-15 years)
  State Lenders → Operate acquired assets (decades)
  Institutional Holders → Accept haircut, move on
  Resource Extractors → Acquire assets at distressed prices
```
