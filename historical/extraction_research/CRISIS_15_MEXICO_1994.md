# MEXICO 1994 — EXTRACTION MAP

## CRISIS SUMMARY
"Tequila Crisis." Peso collapsed in December 1994 after government abandoned crawling peg. Inflation hit 52%. US organized $52B bailout (largest ever at time). Triggered emerging market contagion. Template for future EM crises.

| Factor | Status |
|--------|--------|

| Tesobonos | Dollar-indexed short-term debt, $29B outstanding |
| Political shocks | Colosio assassination (March), Chiapas uprising |
| US interest rates | Rising (drew capital back to US) |

---

## THE COLLAPSE

### 1994 Pressure Building
- **March**: Presidential candidate Colosio assassinated
- **Rising US rates**: Investors preferred safer US returns
- **Capital flight**: Hot money began exiting
- **Peso pressure**: Government spent reserves defending peg

### December 1994
- Reserves nearly exhausted
- Government devalued peso 15% (December 20)
- Markets panicked — demanded more devaluation
- Peso fell further, eventually lost 50%+

### The Death Spiral
- Devaluation → inflation → more devaluation
- Tesobonos (dollar-indexed) became unpayable
- Default imminent without intervention

---

## THE BAILOUT

### January 31, 1995
- **US Treasury**: $20B (Exchange Stabilization Fund)
- **IMF**: $17.8B
- **BIS + G7**: $10B+
- **Total package**: $52B

### Why US Intervened
- NAFTA just launched (1994) — political credibility
- Fear of Mexican default contagion to US banks
- Immigration pressure if Mexican economy collapsed
- Clinton administration pushed through without Congress

---

## STRATEGIC POSITIONING

### Who Exited Early
- US mutual funds began liquidating Mexican assets mid-1994
- Anyone who read the political instability signals
- Smart money converted pesos to dollars before December

### Who Was Trapped
- Mexican businesses with dollar debt
- Retail investors in peso-denominated assets
- Tesobono holders (got bailed out by US package)

### Who Profited
- Those short the peso
- Those who bought distressed Mexican assets post-crisis
- US banks (got repaid via bailout instead of taking losses)

---

## VALUE TRANSFER MAGNITUDE

### Currency Loss
- Peso lost 50%+ of value
- Inflation: 52%
- Real wages collapsed

### Bailout Transfer
- **Tesobono holders**: Made whole via US/IMF money
- **US taxpayer risk**: $20B (eventually repaid with interest)
- **Pattern**: Private losses socialized via sovereign bailout

### Asset Fire Sale
- Post-crisis privatizations and acquisitions
- Foreign capital acquired Mexican assets at distressed prices

## UNIQUE FEATURES

### First Modern EM Bailout
- $52B package was unprecedented scale
- Template for all future EM rescues (Asia 1997, Russia 1998, etc.)
- **Moral hazard debate**: Did bailout encourage future risk-taking?

### Tesobonos as Dollar Liability
- Mexico issued peso debt indexed to dollar
- When peso collapsed, dollar obligation exploded
- **Lesson**: Dollar-indexed domestic debt is a trap

### US Political Stakes
- NAFTA had just launched — Clinton's legacy
- Mexican collapse would embarrass US trade policy
- **Pattern**: Geopolitics drives bailout decisions

### "Tequila Effect"
- Term coined for contagion to other EM markets
- Argentina, Brazil, others affected
- **First named EM contagion event**
