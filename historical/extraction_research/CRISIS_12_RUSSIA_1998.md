# RUSSIA 1998 — EXTRACTION MAP

## CRISIS SUMMARY
GKO (short-term Treasury bill) default. Largest sovereign default in history at the time. Ruble devalued 75%. Triggered LTCM collapse and global contagion. Oligarch formation accelerated — crisis created the modern Russian billionaire class.

## THE CRISIS

### August 17, 1998 — "Black Monday"
- Government defaulted on GKO and OFZ bonds
- Ruble devalued (from 6 to 21 per dollar)
- Banking system collapsed
- **Trigger**: Combination of Asian crisis contagion, low oil prices, fiscal weakness

### Pre-Crisis Dynamics
- GKO yields spiked to 140-290% as panic built
- Foreign hedge funds and speculators had poured in seeking yield
- "Hot money" — speculative capital, not investment

---

## STRATEGIC POSITIONING

### George Soros
- **Pre-crisis**: Predicted Russian collapse
- **August 13, 1998**: Publicly called for currency board regime
- **Role**: More commentator than attacker in Russia (unlike UK 1992, Thailand 1997)

### Foreign Hedge Funds
- Multiple funds had large GKO positions seeking 100%+ yields
- All wiped out in default
- **Lesson**: High yield = high risk was demonstrated violently

### LTCM (Long-Term Capital Management)
- Not directly in Russia, but held positions correlated to Russian risk
- Russian default triggered global deleveraging
- **LTCM loss**: Required $3.6B Fed-organized bailout
- **Pattern**: Sovereign default → hedge fund collapse → systemic risk

---

## THE OLIGARCH OPPORTUNITY

### Who Won
- Russian oligarchs with dollar assets abroad
- Those who could convert rubles to dollars before devaluation
- Those who could acquire distressed assets post-crisis

### Oligarch Positioning
- **July 1998**: Oligarchs hosted birthday party for Chubais (reform architect), "pleaded with him to save their banks"
- **Post-crisis**: Oligarchs acquired state assets at distressed prices
- **Pattern**: Crisis accelerates wealth concentration

### Who Lost
- Ordinary Russians (savings wiped out by devaluation)
- Foreign investors in GKOs (default, no recovery)
- LTCM and correlated funds (liquidation)

---

## VALUE TRANSFER MAGNITUDE

### Domestic Transfer
- **Ruble devaluation**: 75%+ loss for ruble-holders
- **Dollar-holders**: Preserved value, could buy distressed assets
- **Result**: Massive transfer from savers to dollar-rich oligarchs

### Foreign Transfer
- **GKO holders**: Total loss on default
- **LTCM bailout**: $3.6B from major banks
- **Systemic cost**: Global market volatility, contagion

## UNIQUE FEATURES

### Domestic Debt Default
- Unlike most cases (foreign debt), Russia defaulted on domestic obligations
- GKOs were issued in rubles, held largely by domestic banks + foreign speculators
- Default + devaluation = double hit

### Oligarch Formation
- Crisis was inflection point for Russian oligarchy
- Those with dollars could buy assets at cents on dollar
- **Pattern**: Crisis as wealth concentration event

### LTCM Contagion
- Demonstrated that sovereign default could trigger systemic risk
- Fed organized private bailout to prevent broader collapse
- **Lesson**: Sovereign risk is systemic risk
