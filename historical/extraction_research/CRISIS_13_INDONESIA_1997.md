# INDONESIA 1997 — EXTRACTION MAP

## CRISIS SUMMARY
Hardest hit by Asian Financial Crisis. Rupiah lost 85% of value. GDP fell 15%. Banking crisis cost 50%+ of GDP — most expensive in history. Suharto forced out after 31 years. IMF $43B bailout with controversial conditionality.

## THE COLLAPSE

### July-August 1997
- Thai baht collapse triggered regional contagion
- Rupiah came under speculative attack
- **August 1997**: Rupiah at 2,600/dollar

### October 1997 — IMF Arrives
- Government sought IMF assistance
- **Package**: $43B
- **Conditions**: Close 16 banks, cut subsidies, structural reforms

### January 1998 — Rupiah Collapse
- Rupiah crashed to 14,800+/dollar
- 85% devaluation despite IMF program
- **Problem**: IMF conditions worsened panic, didn't restore confidence

### May 1998 — Suharto Falls
- Mass riots following subsidy cuts
- Suharto forced to resign after 31 years
- **Pattern**: IMF conditionality → social unrest → regime change

---

## STRATEGIC POSITIONING

### Who Exited Early
- Foreign investors who saw Thai contagion coming
- Anyone who converted rupiah to dollars before August 1997
- International banks that reduced exposure

### Who Was Trapped
- Domestic corporates with dollar-denominated debt
- Indonesian banks with currency mismatches
- Ordinary citizens (savings, jobs, food prices)

### IMF
- **Role**: Emergency lender with conditions
- **Conditions**: Bank closures, subsidy cuts, structural reform
- **Result**: Conditions accelerated panic (bank closures triggered runs)

---

## VALUE TRANSFER MAGNITUDE

### Currency Transfer
- **Rupiah holders**: Lost 85% of dollar-equivalent value
- **Dollar holders**: Could buy rupiah assets at 85% discount
- **Pattern**: Massive transfer from domestic to foreign holders

### Banking System
- **Cost**: 50%+ of GDP
- **Mechanism**: Government bailed out depositors, absorbed losses
- **Beneficiaries**: Connected depositors rescued; small savers less protected

### Asset Fire Sale
- Post-crisis privatizations at distressed prices
- Foreign investors acquired Indonesian assets cheaply
- **Pattern**: Crisis → foreign acquisition at discount

## UNIQUE FEATURES

### Most Expensive Banking Crisis in History
- 50%+ of GDP consumed by banking sector rescue
- No other crisis has cost this much relative to economy
- **Lesson**: Currency + banking crisis = catastrophic

### IMF Conditionality Backfire
- Closing 16 banks triggered bank runs on remaining banks
- Subsidy cuts triggered riots
- **Lesson**: Textbook reforms can accelerate collapse in panic conditions

### Suharto Fall
- 31-year authoritarian rule ended
- Direct link: IMF conditions → price increases → riots → resignation
- **Pattern**: Sovereign crisis can trigger regime change

### Debt Restructuring Task Force (JITF)
- Created to mediate creditor-debtor negotiations
- Indonesia Bank Restructuring Agency (IBRA) established
- **Pattern**: Crisis creates new institutions
