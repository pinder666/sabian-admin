## THE DEFAULT

### December 2001
- President de la Rúa resigned amid riots
- Convertibility (1:1 peg) abandoned
- Default on all foreign-law bonds
- Largest default in history at the time

### 2005/2010 Restructurings
- **Offer**: ~25-30 cents on the dollar
- **Participation**: 93% of bondholders accepted
- **Holdouts**: 7% refused, litigated

---

## THE VULTURE FUND PLAYBOOK — ORIGIN STORY

### Elliott Management / NML Capital
- **Strategy invented here**: Buy post-default bonds at distressed prices, litigate for full payment
- **Purchase price**: As low as 10 cents on dollar (estimated average 20-25 cents)
- **Face value acquired**: ~$600M+
- **Legal strategy**: Pari passu clause — demand equal treatment with performing bonds

### The Legal Battle (2002-2016)
- **2012**: Judge Griesa ruled Argentina must pay holdouts before paying restructured bondholders
- **Tactic**: Argentina blocked from paying anyone, effectively locked out of markets
- **Seizure attempts**: NML seized Argentine naval vessel in Ghana
- **Settlement (2016)**: Argentina paid $4.65B to all holdouts
- **NML return**: $2.426B on ~$177M cost = **1,270%**

### Other Holdouts
- **Aurelius Capital**: Co-lead with Elliott
- **Other funds**: Various smaller positions

## VALUE TRANSFER MAGNITUDE

### Restructuring Participants (93%)
- **Received**: ~25-30 cents on dollar
- **Loss**: 70-75% of face value

### Holdouts (7%)
- **Purchased at**: 10-30 cents
- **Received**: 100 cents (via litigation)
- **Total payout**: $4.65 billion
- **Elliott return**: 1,270%

### The Math
- If you bought at 10 cents and got 100 cents = 900% return
- If you bought at 25 cents and got 100 cents = 300% return
- Elliott's 1,270% implies very low average purchase price + legal fees recovered

## THE TEMPLATE

Argentina 2001 is the **origin case** for modern vulture fund strategy:

1. **Buy post-default at 10-30 cents**
2. **Refuse all restructuring offers**
3. **Litigate in New York courts**
4. **Argue pari passu (equal treatment)**
5. **Block sovereign from paying anyone**
6. **Wait for settlement at face value**
7. **Return: 300-1,000%+**

Every subsequent vulture play (Peru, Congo, Greece, Zambia) follows this template.

---

## UNIQUE FEATURES

### The Pari Passu Weapon
- Contract clause requiring "equal treatment" of bondholders
- Elliott argued: if you pay restructured bondholders, you must pay us too
- **Judge Griesa agreed**: Argentina blocked from all payments
- This ruling changed sovereign debt forever

### 15-Year Patience
- Elliott bought in 2002, settled in 2016
- Required holding through multiple Argentine governments, elections, legal appeals
- **Lesson**: Vulture funds have infinite patience; sovereigns don't

### Precedent for Future Holdouts
- Every subsequent restructuring (Greece, Ukraine, Zambia) had to consider holdout risk
- CAC (Collective Action Clause) movement started to prevent future Elliott plays
