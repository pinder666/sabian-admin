# GREECE 2012 — EXTRACTION MAP

## CRISIS SUMMARY
Largest sovereign debt restructuring in history at the time. €199.2B in bonds restructured with ~65% NPV haircut. PSI (Private Sector Involvement) completed March 2012. Holdouts paid in full — set dangerous precedent.

## THE PSI RESTRUCTURING

### Terms
- **Nominal haircut**: 53.5%
- **NPV haircut**: ~65%
- **New bonds**: Lower coupon, longer maturities
- **Participation**: ~97% of bondholders

### Collective Action Clauses
- Greece retroactively inserted CACs into domestic-law bonds
- Forced participation by minority holdouts on Greek-law bonds
- Foreign-law bonds: no CACs, holdouts could sue

---

## STRATEGIC POSITIONING (Pre-Stress)

### Hedge Funds Buying Distressed
- Multiple funds accumulated Greek bonds at 60-70 cents pre-PSI
- **Strategy**: Bet on holdout litigation or full payment

---

## THE HOLDOUT PLAY

### Dart Management
- **Strategy**: Accumulated foreign-law bonds at 60-70 cents
- **Result**: Received 100 cents on the dollar
- **Timing**: Payment within one month of PSI completion
- **Return**: ~40-50% profit in months

### The Precedent Problem
- Greece paid Dart first (April 2012): €436 million
- Other holdouts cited "equal treatment"
- **2013 total holdout payments**: €1.7 billion
- **Pattern**: Paying first holdout opened floodgates

### Contemplated Holdouts
- **Elliott Associates**: Reportedly considered holdout strategy
- **Aurelius Capital**: Reportedly considered holdout strategy
- Both ultimately may have participated in PSI or sold

## UNIQUE FEATURES

### The "Precedent Trap"
- Greece paid Dart quickly to avoid litigation
- Other holdouts immediately demanded equal treatment
- **Lesson**: Never pay the first holdout — it guarantees paying all of them

### Eurozone Politics
- First Eurozone restructuring
- ECB bonds explicitly excluded from haircut
- Private bondholders took loss; official sector protected

### CAC Insertion
- Greece retroactively changed bond terms to force participation
- Only worked for Greek-law bonds
- Foreign-law holdouts immune — hence the payouts
