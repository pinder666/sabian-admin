# EXTRACTION PATTERN SIGNATURES

## Overview
Across 15 crises, distinct patterns emerge. These signatures can be detected using Sabian signals + public positioning data.

---

## PATTERN 1: THE VULTURE PLAY
**Seen in**: Argentina 2001, Greece 2012, Peru 1996, Congo 1990s

### Sequence
1. Sovereign defaults on bonds
2. Bond price collapses to 10-30 cents
3. Distressed funds buy quietly
4. Majority bondholders accept restructuring (70%+ haircut)
5. Distressed funds refuse, litigate
6. Court rules pari passu (equal treatment)
7. Sovereign blocked from paying anyone until holdouts paid
8. Settlement at 100 cents after 5-15 years

### Detection Signals (Sabian + Public Data)
- **Sabian**: Score crosses CRITICAL threshold
- **Public**: 13F filings show distressed fund accumulation
- **Public**: Restructuring announced with <100% participation
- **Public**: Court filings in NY/UK

### Timing
- Entry: 0-12 months post-default
- Exit: 5-15 years (requires extreme patience)

### Returns
- Argentina (Elliott): 1,270%
- Greece (Dart): ~40-60%
- Peru (Elliott): ~400%

## PATTERN 2: THE INFRASTRUCTURE TRAP
**Seen in**: Sri Lanka, Zambia, Pakistan, Venezuela, Ecuador

### Sequence
1. State lender (China) provides infrastructure loans during baseline
2. Project underperforms or economy weakens
3. Debt service becomes unsustainable
4. Debt converted to equity or long-term concession
5. Strategic asset transferred to lender

### Detection Signals (Sabian + Public Data)
- **Sabian**: Governance signal weakens (going dark)
- **Sabian**: Economic stress rises while debt service constant
- **Public**: BRI tracker shows large infrastructure loan
- **Public**: Concession renegotiation announced
- **Pattern**: Loan signed during BASELINE, converted during CRITICAL

### Examples
| Country | Asset | Conversion |
|---------|-------|------------|
| Sri Lanka | Hambantota Port | 99-year lease, 70% stake |
| Zambia | Mopani Copper | Sold to state, 3% royalty to Glencore |
| Venezuela | Citgo | 49.9% pledged to Rosneft |
| Ecuador | Oil reserves | 96,000 bpd committed to China |

### Timing
- Loan: 3-10 years before crisis
- Conversion: During or immediately after crisis

## PATTERN 3: THE INSIDER EXIT
**Seen in**: Lebanon 2020, Russia 1998, Indonesia 1997

### Sequence
1. Crisis approaching but not public
2. Connected insiders convert local currency to dollars
3. Capital controls announced (after insiders exit)
4. Ordinary citizens trapped
5. Currency collapses
6. Insiders buy distressed assets with preserved dollars

### Detection Signals
- **Sabian**: Capital flows signal reverses sharply
- **Public**: Large wire transfers to offshore jurisdictions
- **Public**: Capital controls announced (confirms insider exit occurred)
- **Timing**: Insider exit precedes controls by days/weeks

### Lebanon Specific
- October 2019: ~$4B moved out by elites
- October 2019: Capital controls imposed (after the fact)
- Ordinary depositors: Frozen

## PATTERN 4: THE CURRENCY ATTACK
**Seen in**: Thailand 1997, UK 1992, Mexico 1994

### Sequence
1. Currency overvalued, peg unsustainable
2. Short-term debt high, reserves low
3. Hedge funds identify vulnerability
4. Build short positions (borrow currency, sell for dollars)
5. Pressure on central bank reserves
6. Central bank capitulates, floats currency
7. Currency collapses 30-50%+
8. Hedge funds profit

### Detection Signals
- **Sabian**: Capital flows elevated, economic stress rising
- **Public**: CDS spreads widening
- **Public**: Central bank reserve drawdown
- **Public**: Currency futures/options positioning (where visible)
- **Timing**: Attack typically 6-18 months after fundamentals weaken

### Returns
- Soros (UK 1992): $1B
- Soros (Asia 1997): ~$2B

## PATTERN 5: THE OIL-FOR-LOAN TRAP
**Seen in**: Venezuela, Ecuador, Chad

### Sequence
1. Oil-producing country takes commodity-collateralized loan
2. Loan priced at high oil price ($100+)
3. Oil price crashes
4. Country must ship 2-3x more oil to service same debt
5. Domestic revenue collapses
6. Fiscal crisis accelerates
7. More oil committed to pay debt

### Detection Signals
- **Sabian**: Economic stress rises while trade signals stable
- **Public**: Oil export data shows increasing volume to single buyer
- **Public**: Loan terms reveal commodity indexing
- **Pattern**: Commodity price drop + rising export volumes = trap activated

### Venezuela Math
- Loan at $100/barrel: Ship X barrels
- Oil at $40/barrel: Ship 2.5X barrels to make same payment
- Result: Less revenue for everything else

## PATTERN 6: THE HIDDEN DEBT REVEAL
**Seen in**: Mozambique 2016

### Sequence
1. Government takes secret loans (off-budget)
2. Money disappears (corruption, kickbacks)
3. Hidden debt revealed (whistleblower, audit, investigation)
4. IMF/World Bank freeze support
5. Market access lost
6. Economic collapse

### Detection Signals
- **Sabian**: Governance goes dark (reporting stops)
- **Public**: IMF Article IV raises questions about fiscal transparency
- **Public**: Investigative journalism reveals hidden deals
- **Pattern**: Gap between official debt and actual obligations

### Mozambique
- Hidden: $2B in secret loans
- Revealed: 2016
- Cost: 1 year GDP, 2 million into poverty

---

## PATTERN 7: THE CONDITIONALITY BACKFIRE
**Seen in**: Indonesia 1997, Argentina 2001

### Sequence
1. Crisis triggers IMF program
2. IMF imposes conditions (bank closures, subsidy cuts)
3. Conditions accelerate panic (bank runs, riots)
4. Social/political instability
5. Regime change
6. New government inherits mess

### Detection Signals
- **Sabian**: Score in CRITICAL + IMF program announced
- **Public**: Conditionality terms leaked/announced
- **Public**: Protests/riots following price increases
- **Pattern**: IMF conditions → social unrest within weeks

### Indonesia
- October 1997: IMF announces 16 bank closures
- Result: Bank runs on surviving banks
- May 1998: Suharto forced out after 31 years

## COMBINED SIGNATURE: MAXIMUM EXTRACTION

When multiple patterns occur simultaneously:

```
1. Pre-crisis: State lender positions (Pattern 2)
2. Crisis onset: Insiders exit (Pattern 3)
3. Currency attacked (Pattern 4)
4. Default occurs
5. Vulture funds enter (Pattern 1)
6. IMF conditions imposed (Pattern 7)
7. Assets transferred to positioned actors (Pattern 2)
8. Vultures litigate for years (Pattern 1)
```

**Countries experiencing 4+ patterns**: Argentina, Venezuela, Sri Lanka
