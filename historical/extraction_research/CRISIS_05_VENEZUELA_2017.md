# VENEZUELA 2017 — EXTRACTION MAP

## CRISIS SUMMARY
PDVSA (state oil company) and sovereign bonds under extreme stress. Not formal default in 2017, but effective exclusion from markets. Oil-for-loan arrangements with Russia and China became debt traps. Citgo (US refinery) pledged as collateral to Russia.

## STRATEGIC POSITIONING (Pre-Stress)

### Rosneft (Russian State Oil)
- **2015 onward**: Provided ~$6.5 billion in loans to PDVSA
- **January 2017**: Venezuela pledged 49.9% stake in Citgo as collateral
- **Pattern**: Cash injection → strategic asset collateral → potential seizure

### China Development Bank / Exim
- **2007-2017**: $50B+ in oil-for-loan arrangements
- **Terms**: Loans negotiated when oil was $100+/barrel
- **Trap**: When oil fell to $30-50, Venezuela had to ship 2-3x more oil to service same debt
- **Shipments**: ~1/3 of PDVSA exports went to China and Russia

---

## OIL-FOR-LOAN TRAP MECHANICS

### The Math
- Loans priced at $100/barrel oil
- Oil crashes to $40/barrel
- Venezuela must ship 2.5x more oil to make same payment
- Leaves less oil revenue for domestic budget
- Accelerates fiscal collapse

### Debt Service vs. Export Revenue
> "The fall in crude prices made the oil-for-loan agreements more onerous because loan payments were negotiated when crude prices were higher, requiring PDVSA to ship more oil to continue servicing the debts at the same rate."

---

## INSTITUTIONAL EXIT
- Western banks and bondholders largely exited or wrote down
- US sanctions (2017 onward) limited new financing
- Russia and China became lenders of last resort — at extractive terms

---

## DISTRESSED ENTRY

### Russia as Strategic Creditor
- **2016-2017**: "Caracas reportedly used Russian cash to avoid defaulting on sovereign bond payments on two separate occasions"
- **Pattern**: Emergency liquidity → collateral extraction

### Citgo Collateral Risk
> "Escalation into a commercial dispute with Chinese or Russian firms demanding prompt payments could affect Venezuela by triggering default clauses on bonds, or lead PDVSA to lose control of U.S. subsidiary Citgo."

---

## RESOURCE/ASSET CAPTURE

### Citgo Petroleum
- **Asset**: US-based refining and retail network (3 refineries, pipelines, gas stations)
- **Pledged to**: Rosneft (Russian state)
- **Stake**: 49.9%
- **Status**: US government blocked transfer due to sanctions, but collateral claim remains

### Oil Shipments
- **To China**: ~500,000 bpd committed
- **To Russia**: Major shipment commitments
- **Pattern**: Future oil production pledged as debt service — resource extraction without ownership transfer

## VALUE TRANSFER MAGNITUDE
- **Citgo stake value**: ~$3-5B
- **Oil shipment commitments**: $20B+ over loan lifetimes
- **Strategic value**: Russia gains leverage over US refining infrastructure; China secures long-term oil supply

## UNIQUE FEATURES

### Geopolitical Extraction
- Unlike other crises, the "extractors" are nation-states (Russia, China), not hedge funds
- Strategic assets (Citgo, oil reserves) are the prize, not just financial returns
- US sanctions created three-way competition: US blocks Russia from seizing Citgo

### Oil-for-Loan as Debt Trap
- Loans denominated in dollars, repaid in oil
- When oil price falls, real debt burden multiplies
- Venezuela trapped: must ship more oil, less revenue for domestic needs
